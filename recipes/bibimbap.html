Bibimbap Recipe

This bibimbap recipe makes a popular and delicious Korean meal. Traditionally, bibimbap was eaten on the eve of the Lunar New Year, to use up any leftovers before the start of the new year.

It's a bowl mixed rice, bibimbap is a delicious rice bowl topped with vegetables, beef, a whole egg, and gochujang (red chili pepper paste). It takes approximately 30 minutes to prep and 30 minutes to cook netting a total serving time of an hour.

Ingrediants for one serving:

  • Cucumbers
  • Gochujang
  • Vegetables
  • Soy Sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Seasonings of fresh garlic and dried red pepper flakes
  • Thinly Sliced Beef
  • One Egg
  • Rice
  • Sesame Oil
  • Sesame Seeds

Steps:

  1. Stir the cucumber pieces and gochujang together in a bowl.
  2. Cook the spinach until bright green and wilted.
  3. Drain the spinach, squeeze out as much moisture as possible, and set aside.
  4. Cook the carrots until soft, then stir in the cucumber mixture and red pepper flakes.
  5. Brown the beef in a different skillet and set aside.
  6. Fry the eggs until the yolks are runny, but whites are firm.
  7. Top each serving of rice with the spinach mixture, beef, and the cucumber mixture.
  8. Place an egg atop each serving, then drizzle with sesame oil, sesame seeds, and gochujang (if desired).
